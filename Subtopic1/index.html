MANAGING YOUR STUDIES

Time Breakdown

Time management is an integral part of any major task, including university studies. Balancing between studies, work, social and life commitments can appear challenging, but with effective time management this balance can be achieved.

Breaking down the time spent on activities can go a long way. It allows measurement for how much time is being allocated to a task, and if that time amount needs to be adjusted. From there a daily, weekly, and even semester long schedule can be produced to assist in handling assessments, but also visualize how much time there is available for both studies and personal time.

Each of the following sections should be used in conjunction with one another to achieve an effective time planner for an entire semester of studies.

Time Inventory

Breaking down the time required for each daily activity in life can help effectively visualize how much time is spent on an activity, and how much actual time there is available for personal activities and studying. This helps analyse just how much time is available but it can also be adjusted to include unscheduled events.

Time Management Calculator

A detailed breakdown of the time inventory. It shows the amount of time allocated towards each activity and reveals if too much time is being spent on certain activities such as leisure. University studies require dedication and an appropriate amount of time should be allocated towards achieving results.

Weekly timetable

A weekly breakdown helps keep track of time available for studies and personal activities. By filling in essential commitments (lectures, work, etc.) the remaining time available is shown. Tailor the rest of the time to when studying is personally effective. The timetable should be adjusted for any upcoming events, or times of intense study, such as a major assessment or examinations.

Semester plan

Planning out the semester helps visualize and assess the amount of time needed for an assessment. It will also show choke points when there are multiple assessments due, and if more time should be allocated towards studies during those periods.
